Core Investment Philosophies

  1. Buying a company's stocks is equivalent to buying the whole company.
  2. The value of a company depends on its discounted future free cash flows.
  3. Look for securities with prices that are unjustifiably low based on their intrinsic worth.
  4. In the short run, the market is a voting machine but in the long run it is a weighing machine.
  5. Restrict investments to businesses that can easily be analyzed.
  6. We will stick to businesses whose profit picture for decades to come seems reasonably predictable.
  7. Avoid: companies with poor economic characteristics; businesses we do not understand; companies burdened with high debt; securities with no margin of safety.
